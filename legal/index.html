Transparency is the foundation.

Required disclosures, entity structure, and licensing information for BidZen LLC and Shrayer Law Firm LLC.

Entity Disclosure — Florida Bar Rule 4-5.7

BidZen LLC (FL Broker Lic. CQ1060113) provides real estate brokerage services only. Brokerage services do not create an attorney-client relationship and are not subject to attorney-client privilege.

Legal services are provided solely by Shrayer Law Firm LLC under a separate engagement, separate fees, and separate terms. Glen Shrayer, Esq. (FL Bar #0057253) is the licensed broker of BidZen LLC and managing attorney of Shrayer Law Firm LLC — these are distinct entities. Use of BidZen's brokerage services does not constitute engagement of Shrayer Law Firm LLC or create any legal representation.

Brokerage Licensing

BidZen LLC is a licensed Florida real estate brokerage. Florida Real Estate Broker License No. CQ1060113. BidZen LLC provides real estate brokerage services only.

All brokerage transactions are conducted in compliance with Florida Statutes Chapter 475 and applicable Florida Real Estate Commission (FREC) rules. BidZen LLC is an Equal Housing Opportunity brokerage.

Legal Services Licensing

Legal services are provided by Shrayer Law Firm LLC. Managing Attorney: Glen Shrayer, Esq., Florida Bar No. 0057253. Licensed to practice law in Florida. Also licensed in New York, Massachusetts, and Michigan.

Shrayer Law Firm LLC operates independently of BidZen LLC. Engagement of legal services requires a separate retainer agreement and creates an attorney-client relationship governed by the Florida Rules of Professional Conduct.

For legal services inquiries: shrayerlaw.com

Attorney-Client Privilege

Real estate brokerage services provided by BidZen LLC do not create an attorney-client relationship and communications with BidZen LLC in its brokerage capacity are not protected by attorney-client privilege.

When clients separately engage Shrayer Law Firm LLC for legal representation under a distinct engagement agreement, those legal communications are protected by attorney-client privilege. Brokerage services and legal services are provided under separate terms and fee structures.

The distinction between brokerage services and legal services is required by Florida Bar Rule 4-5.7 governing lawyers who provide non-legal services.
